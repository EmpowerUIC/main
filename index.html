Meet the Candidates


President

Matthew

Matthew Almendras is a 3rd-year public health major with a concentration in health policy and is on the pre-law track. As the current serving President of the Undergraduate Student Government (USG), Matthew has led many initiatives that improved our UIC campus and the student experience. He started out as Interim Speaker of the House, and in the Spring 2021 semester, became Chief of Staff. He then served as the Director of Student Success where he has worked to improve career resources, advocated for improved course programs, and implemented two textbook affordability programs. Initially, USG was supposed to be a side hobby for Matthew to observe the inner workings of UIC, but he soon realized that community service and public policy was his passion, and loved his work at USG.Next year, Matthew seeks to continue his work in textbook affordability, helping food insecure students, and expanding and promoting opportunities for students to be successful. Outside of USG, Matthew is the Treasurer of the Chess Club at UIC, and a Director at the Chicago Chess Center. He currently is a Government and Community Relations Intern for the Chicago Transit Authority (CTA). For fun Matthew loves playing chess and basketball, as well as keeping up with current events.



Vice President

Michael

Michael Fernandes is a 3rd year GPPA Medical Scholars student majoring in Political Science and minoring in public policy. As the current Vice President, Michael Fernandes worked hard to expand inclusivity on campus through expanding religious exemptions and better practices on campus. In addition, he advocated for a full-week Fall break to the point where the Senate Executive Committee at UIC is considering implementing this for Fall 2024. Michael Fernandes also passed a resolution that reenacts the Library Shuttle for UIC students to use starting at 9pm. Michael will continue to look into ways to improve student safety on campus and to facilitate the success of students. Michael Fernandes wants to add 24/7 mental health counseling to campus, finish his work on expanding Fall break to a week, and support President Matthew Almendras to implement the Meal Swipe Donation Program. With your support, Michael will help ensure our student population is healthy and prepared for personal and academic success.



Student Trustee

Mohammed

Mohammed Haq is a 3rd-year honors college student majoring in biological sciences and on the pre-dentistry career pathway. As the current Student Member of the Board of Trustees, Mohammed represents more than 35,000 students at UIC to the Board of Trustees, who oversee the broader University of Illinois System. In his term so far, Mohammed has focused his efforts on re-establishing the office of student trustee as a central hub that advances the most pertinent undergraduate and graduate student interests. Alongside his peers in the Undergraduate Student Government, Graduate Student Council, Health Professions Student Council, and the Student Bar Association, Mohammed has worked on behalf of the student body in advocating for affordability, faculty diversity, representative administrative leadership, and campus safety.

About the Platform

Our Voice

We will put the average student first by advocating for and providing resources that empower you to reach your full potential!

Prioritizing Mental Health

- Ensuring the new head of the counseling center puts students first.

- Advocating for increased investment, more counseling staff, and sponsoring more workshops and outreach

Improving Food Options on Campus

- UIC is selecting new restaurants and catering provider. We will advocate for high quality and affordable options that students love.

- Advocate for more vegan, vegetarian, and halal options.

Improving Vacation and Time Off

- Advocate for UIC to adopt a Thanksgiving Break model similar to UIUC

- Advocate to bring Reading Days to UIC.

Our Communities

This year as we returned to campus and the pandemic began to fade, we have worked to reignite our campus community. This year through events such as the UIC Tailgate and Connecting Student Leaders, we've made major strides. We will continue this work by empowering clubs through funding, continuing to bring Greek life together, and advocating for programs that student demand. Safety has been a corner stone of the work we have conducted from the creation of the Safety Task Force to advocating for and establishing improved safety on West Campus.


- Put student safety first and continue to advocate to impliment student-backed recommendations

- Improve student housing and empower student workers.

- Elevate our campus experience.

- Advocate for a fair contract for our graduate student workers.

Our Futures

The cost of education is one of the largest burdens on our generation. The Empower candidates have spent the past year dedicating over $3,000 investing in Open Education Resources (OER). We plan to continue this work if elected by increasing this investment and continuing to invest in and advocate for lower textbook costs. With a seat on both the USG and Board of Trustees, we will fight to keep fees and tuition low. We will work to preventing increases that continue to raise costs in the future.


- Ensure students are competitive in the job market through promoting career development resources. Over the past year we have hosted networking events and career workshops to help empower student professionally. We will continue this work and leverage UIC's connections to Chicago's business community.

- Increase access to scholarship resources and advocate for increased investment.
